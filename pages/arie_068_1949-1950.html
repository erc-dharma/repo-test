ARIE/1949-1950/A/1949-50/2 (page 10)

Do. (Findspot: Dubi)
Dynasty
Bhaumas or Nārakas of Assam.
King
Bhāskaravarman
Date
…
Language
Sanskrit in early Northern Characters
Remarks
Registers a renewal, by king Bhāskaravarman, of a grant of land formerly made by king Bhūtivarman to Priyaṅkara-Ghōshasvāmin and other Brāhmaṇas of various gōtras and sūtras. A list of the new donees who were apparently descended from the earlier recipients is given. Bhāskara-varman's genealogy is traced from Naraka, born of Vishṇu and Bhūdēvī. He is stated to be the younger brother of Supratishṭhitavarman and both the brothers are stated to have fought a sanguinary battle against the Gauḍas in which they were defeated and taken prisoners but regained their kingdom later. Published in Epigraphia Indica, Vol. XXX, No. 47.

Śrīdhāraṇarāta, lord of Samataṭa
Date
Year 8, Śrāvaṇa śu. di. 9.
Language
Sanskrit in early East Indian Characters
Remarks
Issued from Dēvaparvata. Registers the royal gift of lands in the vishayas of Guptīnāṭana and Paṭalāyikā for the ex-penses of worship and offerings to the Buddha (Tathāgata-ratna or the Ratnatraya), for the maintenance of the Ārya-saṅgha engaged in the Buddhist liturgy and for the per-formance of the Pañcha-mahāyajña by Brāhmaṇas. The gift is stated to have been made by the king, the oral orders to the effect being conveyed by (his son ?) Yuvarā a Śrī- Baladhāraṇarāta, on the representation made by the Mahā-sandhivigrahādhikṛita Jayanātha. Śrīdhāraṇarāta is des-cribed as the son of Jīvadhāraṇarāta-bhaṭṭāraka, called prāptapañcha-mahāśabda and the lord of Samataṭa and other dēśas. The order conveying the gift was issued by the kumārāmātyas and the adhikaraṇas of Dēvaparvata, also mentioned on the seal of the grant wherein they are described as devoted to the feet of the lord of Samataṭa. The order is addressed to the visha-yapatis of Guptīnāṭana and Paṭalāyika. Published in Ind. Hist. Quart., Vol. XXII, pp. 221 ff. and Plates.
