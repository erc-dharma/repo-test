Remarks
Begins with a genealogical account of the king whose descent is traced from Nalla Bhīma of the solar race and Kāśyapa-gōtra. Registers the gift by the king to several Brāhmaṇas of the village of Peṇṭapāḍu with two hamlets renamed Gaṅgādharapura. The king is stated to have been installed by Prōlaya Nāyaka, son of Pōchi Nāyaka, as governor over the territory of Vēṅga Bhūpati his maternal uncle, on the latter's death in battle against the Muslims. This territory is stated to have consisted of Chintapeṇṭa, Vēṅgi and Goṇṭūru included in Chintalapūṇi. Further the gift of the village is stated to have been made by Bhaktirāja after obtaining the consent of Vīra Vōbi Nāyaka, son of Prōlaya Nāyaka.
